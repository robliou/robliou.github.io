My journey to full-stack developer

Born and raised in California, I began my career in the San Francisco Bay Area as an auditor/ CPA for 6 years, the majority of it with a Big 4 accounting firm. After business school in the Midwest, I moved to China and entered the oil and gas industry, working primarily in upstream research and consulting for a well-known firm. Many of the projects we did required extensive data mining and acquisition in order to construct the models that would later be useful for clients.

Towards the end of 2019, in my position as Research Manager for IMA, my projects begin to veer into data science, which necessitated my learning how to code. I took a strong liking to R, with my favorite data science project as building multi-variate regression models in order to find correlations between variables and make forecasts.

I am passionate about full-stack web engineering and making technology useful for everyday considerations.

In early 2021, I entered the NEO blockchain hackathon, with the idea to decentralize data and research. My submission was built with HTML/CSS and a little bit of Javascript. For my efforts and presentation, my project ended up as one of the 12 winners (out of 90+ entrants) selected for incubation. Learning full-stack software engineering along the way (with the help of Codecademy and other sites), I released DDR to a succesful launch in March of 2022.
